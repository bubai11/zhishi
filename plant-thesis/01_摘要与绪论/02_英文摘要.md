# Abstract

## Abstract

With the growing awareness of biodiversity conservation and the rapid development of Web technologies, it has become increasingly important to present botanical knowledge to the public in a more intuitive and interactive way. Traditional plant information systems often suffer from scattered data sources, limited visualization capabilities, and insufficient support for popular science communication, making it difficult to balance data accuracy, visual presentation, and user engagement.

This paper designs and implements a plant popular science visualization and interaction system based on multi-source data fusion. The back end of the system is built with Node.js, Express, Sequelize, and MySQL, while the front end is implemented with React, TypeScript, and Vite under a front-end and back-end separated architecture. At the data level, the system integrates crawled data, open botanical data, and manually curated resources, and organizes plant basic information, taxonomy, distribution, conservation status, and image assets into a unified data framework for popular science applications.

In terms of functional design, the system takes plant knowledge browsing as the foundation, visualization analysis as the key feature, and interactive learning as an auxiliary enhancement. It supports plant search, plant detail display, taxonomic exploration, regional distribution analysis, hotspot presentation, and quiz-based learning interaction. In particular, the system emphasizes the visual expression of taxonomic structures, spatial distribution, and statistical results, so that users can understand plant knowledge from multiple perspectives. In addition, user behaviors such as browsing, bookmarking, recommended quizzes, and review feedback are connected to improve engagement and continuity of learning.

System testing results show that the core functions run stably, the front-end and back-end cooperate properly, and the system can meet the design objectives of plant popular science presentation, knowledge understanding, and visualization interaction. The work of this paper can provide a reference for the design and implementation of similar plant popular science systems based on multi-source data integration.

Key Words: plant popular science; multi-source data fusion; visualization interaction; plant knowledge presentation; Web system
